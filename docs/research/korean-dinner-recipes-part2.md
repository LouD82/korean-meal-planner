# Korean Dinner Recipes Research (Part 2)

## Research Scope
- Research focused on additional Korean dinner recipes suitable for batch cooking
- Specifically looking for recipes that are:
  - Easy to prepare (minimal cooking steps)
  - Suitable for batch cooking on Sundays
  - Can be stored and reheated throughout the week
  - Use ingredients commonly available at H-Mart
  - Different from the previously documented recipes (Korean Beef Bowl, Korean Beef Bulgogi, and Kimchi Jjigae)
  - More substantial for dinner options

## Key Findings
- Korean soups and stews are particularly well-suited for batch cooking and reheating
- Many traditional Korean dishes are designed to be made in large quantities and consumed over several days
- Dishes with rich broths maintain moisture and flavor when reheated
- Proper storage techniques are crucial for maintaining food quality throughout the week
- Some recipes can be portioned and frozen for even longer storage
- Recipes can often be modified to include additional vegetables for a more balanced meal

### Recipe 1: Yukgaejang (육개장) - Spicy Beef and Vegetable Soup

#### Recipe Name
Yukgaejang (육개장) - Spicy Beef and Vegetable Soup

#### Brief Description
Yukgaejang is a spicy, hearty Korean soup made with shredded beef, vegetables, and a rich, smoky broth. This nutritious soup features tender beef brisket, fernbrake (gosari), bean sprouts, and green onions, all seasoned with Korean chili flakes for a warming heat. It's traditionally made in large batches, making it perfect for meal prep, and the flavors actually improve over time as they meld together.

#### Preparation Time
30 minutes

#### Cooking Time
1 hour 30 minutes

#### Ingredients
**For the broth and beef:**
- 1 pound beef brisket, cut into several pieces along the grain (3 inches long)
- 4 dried shiitake mushrooms
- 1 medium onion, cut in half
- 12 cups water

**For the vegetables:**
- 12 ounces (about 3 cups) mung bean sprouts, washed and strained
- 3-4 large green onions or 14-16 regular green onions, cut into 2½ inch long pieces
- 6 ounces of soaked (or fresh) gosari (fernbrake), cut into 2½ inch long pieces
- 8 cloves of garlic, minced

**For the seasoning sauce:**
- ¼ cup Korean hot pepper flakes (gochugaru)
- 1 tablespoon kosher salt
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon toasted sesame oil
- 1 tablespoon olive oil (or any cooking oil)

**For serving:**
- Steamed white rice
- Extra kosher salt (optional, for individual taste adjustment)

#### Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions
1. **Prepare the broth:**
   - In a large pot, bring 12 cups of water to a boil.
   - Add the beef brisket pieces, dried shiitake mushrooms, and halved onion.
   - Cook over medium-high heat for 1 hour.

2. **While the broth cooks, prepare the seasoning and vegetables:**
   - In a bowl, combine the Korean hot pepper flakes, kosher salt, soy sauce, toasted sesame oil, and olive oil. Mix well and set aside.
   - In a large bowl, combine the mung bean sprouts, green onions, gosari, and minced garlic.

3. **Check the beef:**
   - After 1 hour, check if the beef is tender by trying to split a piece with your fingers or a fork.
   - If it splits easily, it's done. If still tough, cook for an additional 10 minutes.
   - Maintain about 10 cups of broth by adding water if necessary.

4. **Prepare the soup:**
   - Remove the beef, mushrooms, and onion from the broth using a slotted strainer.
   - Discard the cooked onion.
   - Let the beef and mushrooms cool slightly.
   - Mix the vegetables with the seasoning sauce by hand until well incorporated.
   - Add the seasoned vegetables to the boiling broth.
   - Cover and cook for 20 minutes over medium-high heat until the vegetables are tender but not mushy.

5. **Finish the soup:**
   - Slice the mushrooms.
   - Pull the beef apart into strips.
   - Add the shredded beef and sliced mushrooms back to the soup.
   - Cook for another 10 minutes.
   - Remove from heat and serve hot with rice.
   - Provide a small bowl of salt on the table for anyone who wants to adjust the seasoning to their taste.

#### Storage Instructions
- Cool the soup completely before storing.
- Store in airtight containers in the refrigerator for 3-4 days.
- For longer storage, the soup can be kept for up to a week if reheated every other day.
- Alternatively, divide into individual portions and freeze for up to 2-3 months.
- Make sure to leave some space at the top of containers when freezing, as the soup will expand.

#### Reheating Instructions
- **Refrigerated soup:**
  - Reheat on the stovetop over medium heat until hot throughout, about 5-7 minutes.
  - Add a small amount of water if the soup has thickened too much during storage.
  - Stir occasionally while reheating to ensure even heating.
  
- **Frozen soup:**
  - Thaw overnight in the refrigerator for best results.
  - Alternatively, use the defrost function on your microwave.
  - Once thawed, reheat on the stovetop over medium heat until hot throughout.
  - Add fresh green onions as garnish after reheating for a burst of flavor and color.

#### Nutritional Information
- Calories: Approximately 320 per serving
- Protein: 25g
- Carbohydrates: 15g
- Fat: 18g
- Fiber: 4g
- Sodium: 850mg

### Recipe 2: Doenjang Jjigae (된장찌개) - Soybean Paste Stew

#### Recipe Name
Doenjang Jjigae (된장찌개) - Soybean Paste Stew

#### Brief Description
Doenjang Jjigae is a staple Korean stew made with fermented soybean paste (doenjang). This hearty, comforting dish features a rich umami broth with tender pork and a variety of vegetables. It's one of the most popular everyday home-cooked Korean dishes and makes for a satisfying dinner option. The distinct aroma and deep flavor of the fermented soybean paste creates a nourishing meal that's perfect for batch cooking and reheating throughout the week.

#### Preparation Time
20 minutes

#### Cooking Time
15 minutes

#### Ingredients
**Main ingredients:**
- 1/2 pound pork belly or shoulder, sliced into thin strips
- 1 block (14 oz) firm tofu, cut into 1-inch cubes
- 1 small Korean radish (mu) or 1 medium potato, cut into thin small squares
- 1 medium zucchini, cut into 1-inch cubes
- 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
- 2 green onions, roughly chopped
- 1-2 green chili peppers, thinly sliced (optional)
- 1-2 red chili peppers, thinly sliced (optional)
- 8 ounces mushrooms (shiitake, button, or oyster), sliced

**For the broth:**
- 3 tablespoons Korean soybean paste (doenjang)
- 1 tablespoon Korean chili pepper flakes (gochugaru), adjust to taste
- 1 tablespoon minced garlic
- 2 teaspoons sesame oil
- 3 cups water or anchovy broth (for more flavor)
- 1 teaspoon vinegar (optional, to brighten flavors)

#### Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions
1. **Prepare the ingredients:**
   - Cut the tofu and zucchini into about 1-inch cubes.
   - Cut the radish into thin small squares.
   - Thinly slice the onion and peppers.
   - Roughly chop the scallion.
   - Slice the meat into thin strips.

2. **Start cooking:**
   - Preheat a small pot with a little bit of oil.
   - Sauté the meat, soybean paste, and chili pepper flakes over medium heat for 3-4 minutes.

3. **Add the broth:**
   - Add the water (or anchovy broth) and stir well to dissolve the soybean paste.
   - Add the radish (if using potato, add it in the next step).
   - Boil over medium-high heat for 3-4 minutes.

4. **Add remaining vegetables:**
   - Add the potato (if using instead of radish), onion, zucchini, mushrooms, and half of the green onion.
   - Continue to boil for an additional 5-6 minutes until the vegetables are cooked but still firm.

5. **Finish the stew:**
   - Add the tofu, chili peppers, and remaining green onion.
   - Gently stir and cook for another 3-4 minutes.
   - Add the sesame oil and optional vinegar.
   - Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.

#### Storage Instructions
- Cool the stew completely before storing.
- Store in airtight containers in the refrigerator for up to 4-5 days.
- The flavors will continue to develop over time, often making it even more delicious on the second or third day.
- Not recommended for freezing as the texture of tofu can change when frozen and thawed.

#### Reheating Instructions
- **Stovetop method (preferred):**
  - Transfer to a pot and reheat over medium heat until hot throughout, about 5-7 minutes.
  - Stir occasionally to ensure even heating.
  - Add a small amount of water if the stew has thickened too much during storage.

- **Microwave method:**
  - Place in a microwave-safe container.
  - Cover loosely and heat on high for 2-3 minutes, stirring halfway through.
  - Check temperature and heat for additional 30-second intervals if needed.

#### Nutritional Information
- Calories: Approximately 380 per serving
- Protein: 22g
- Carbohydrates: 18g
- Fat: 25g
- Fiber: 5g
- Sodium: 780mg
(Values are approximate and may vary based on specific ingredients used)
