# Korean Lunch Recipes Research (Part 1)

## Research Scope
- Research focused on Korean lunch recipes suitable for batch cooking
- Specifically looking for recipes that are:
  - Easy to prepare (minimal cooking steps)
  - Suitable for batch cooking on Sundays
  - Can be stored and reheated throughout the week
  - Use ingredients commonly available at H-Mart

## Key Findings
- Many Korean side dishes (banchan) are designed to be made in large batches and consumed over several days
- Certain Korean main dishes are particularly well-suited for batch cooking and reheating
- Recipes with sauces tend to maintain flavor and moisture when reheated
- Dishes with bite-sized pieces cook more evenly and reheat better than large cuts

## Detailed Research

### Recipe 1: Dakgangjeong (Sweet Crispy Korean Fried Chicken)

#### Recipe Name
Dakgangjeong (닭강정) - Sweet Crispy Korean Fried Chicken

#### Brief Description
Dakgangjeong is a deep-fried crispy chicken dish glazed in a sticky, sweet, and spicy sauce. This recipe uses bite-sized boneless chicken pieces, making it cook up quickly and perfect for batch preparation. The dish is a popular part of Korean fried chicken culture and stays crispy even after storage.

#### Preparation Time
30 minutes (including optional soaking in milk)

#### Cooking Time
20-25 minutes

#### Ingredients
- 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs (cut into bite-sized pieces)
- 1/2 cup milk (optional for soaking)
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon minced ginger
- 1/2 teaspoon minced garlic
- 1/2 cup potato starch
- Oil for frying

**For the sauce:**
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 3 tablespoons rice vinegar (or white vinegar)
- 3 tablespoons sugar (or corn syrup for extra stickiness)
- 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean red chili pepper paste) - can be reduced or replaced with ketchup for less spice
- 1 tablespoon minced garlic
- 1 teaspoon minced ginger
- 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
- Optional garnish: chopped peanuts, sesame seeds, or sliced green onions

#### Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions
1. **Prepare the chicken:**
   - Remove any visible fat from chicken thighs and cut into bite-sized pieces
   - Optionally soak the chicken in milk for at least 30 minutes in the refrigerator
   - Drain the milk and pat the chicken pieces dry
   - Season with salt, ginger, and garlic

2. **Coat and fry the chicken:**
   - Coat the chicken pieces in potato starch, shaking off excess
   - Heat oil to 330°F in a deep pot
   - Fry the chicken in batches, being careful not to overcrowd
   - Fry for 3-4 minutes until light golden brown
   - Remove and drain on paper towels
   - Allow oil to reheat between batches

3. **Double fry the chicken:**
   - Increase oil temperature to 350°F
   - Fry the chicken pieces again in batches for 2-3 minutes until golden brown and crispy
   - Drain on paper towels

4. **Prepare the sauce:**
   - In a small saucepan, combine all sauce ingredients
   - Bring to a boil over medium heat
   - Reduce heat and simmer for 3-4 minutes until slightly thickened

5. **Combine and serve:**
   - Toss the fried chicken in the hot sauce to coat evenly
   - Garnish with chopped peanuts, sesame seeds, or sliced green onions if desired

#### Storage Instructions
- Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days
- For best results, store the chicken and sauce separately if preparing in advance
- The recipe can be easily doubled or tripled for larger batches

#### Reheating Instructions
- Reheat in a preheated 350°F oven for 10-15 minutes until hot and crispy
- Alternatively, reheat in an air fryer at 350°F for 5-7 minutes
- If stored separately, reheat chicken first, then toss with reheated sauce
- Avoid microwave reheating as it can make the chicken soggy

#### Nutritional Information
- Calories: Approximately 320 per serving
- Protein: 25g
- Carbohydrates: 20g
- Fat: 15g
- Sodium: 650mg

### Recipe 2: Kimchi Mandu (Korean Dumplings)

#### Recipe Name
Kimchi Mandu (김치 만두) - Korean Kimchi Dumplings

#### Brief Description
Kimchi Mandu are Korean dumplings filled with a spicy kimchi mixture. They are highly popular in Korea and can be prepared in advance and frozen, making them perfect for batch cooking. These dumplings can be steamed, pan-fried, or added to soups for versatile meal options throughout the week.

#### Preparation Time
45 minutes

#### Cooking Time
10-15 minutes (varies by cooking method)

#### Ingredients
**For the filling:**
- 1 cup finely chopped kimchi (well-fermented)
- 1/2 pound ground pork (can substitute with beef or mushrooms for vegetarian option)
- 1/2 block firm tofu (about 7 ounces), drained and crumbled
- 1 cup bean sprouts, blanched, chopped and squeezed dry
- 2 ounces sweet potato starch noodles (dangmyeon), soaked, cooked and chopped
- 1/2 medium onion, finely chopped and squeezed dry
- 2 scallions, finely chopped
- 1 tablespoon minced garlic
- 1 teaspoon grated ginger
- 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes) - optional, adjust to taste
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- Salt and pepper to taste

**For assembly:**
- 35-40 dumpling wrappers (store-bought or homemade)
- Small bowl of water for sealing

**For dipping sauce (optional):**
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 1 teaspoon vinegar
- 1/2 teaspoon sesame oil
- Pinch of black pepper
- Chopped scallions

#### Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions
1. **Prepare the filling:**
   - Finely chop the kimchi and squeeze out excess liquid
   - Blanch bean sprouts in boiling water, drain, chop and squeeze out water
   - Squeeze water from tofu using a cheesecloth
   - Finely chop the noodles, onion (squeeze out water), and scallions
   - Mix all filling ingredients in a large bowl until well combined

2. **Assemble the dumplings:**
   - Place a dumpling wrapper on a clean surface
   - Add about 1 tablespoon of filling to the center
   - Wet the edges of the wrapper with water
   - Fold in half to form a half-moon shape
   - Pinch edges firmly to seal
   - For soup dumplings, bring the ends together to form a round shape

3. **Cook the dumplings (choose one method):**
   - **Steaming:** Steam over boiling water for 8-10 minutes
   - **Pan-frying:** Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a pan, add dumplings and cook until bottom is golden brown (2-3 minutes), add 1/4 cup water, cover and steam for 4-5 minutes
   - **Boiling:** Drop dumplings in boiling water and cook for 5-6 minutes until they float

4. **Prepare dipping sauce:**
   - Mix all sauce ingredients in a small bowl

#### Storage Instructions
- Freeze uncooked dumplings on a baking sheet until solid (about 1-2 hours)
- Transfer to airtight containers or freezer bags
- Can be stored frozen for up to 3 months
- Cooked dumplings can be refrigerated for 3-4 days in airtight containers

#### Reheating Instructions
- **For frozen uncooked dumplings:** Cook directly from frozen, adding 1-2 minutes to cooking time
- **For refrigerated cooked dumplings:**
  - Steam for 3-4 minutes
  - Pan-fry with a little oil for 2-3 minutes until crispy and heated through
  - Microwave for 1-2 minutes (convenient but texture will be softer)
- **For soup:** Add directly to hot broth and simmer for 2-3 minutes until heated through

#### Nutritional Information
- Calories: Approximately 45-50 per dumpling
- Protein: 2g
- Carbohydrates: 6g
- Fat: 1.5g
- Sodium: 120mg
- (Values are approximate and may vary based on specific ingredients used)
