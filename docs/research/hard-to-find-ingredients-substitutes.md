# Hard-to-Find Korean Ingredients and Substitutes

This document provides information about potentially hard-to-find Korean ingredients required for the recipes in our meal planner project, along with suitable substitutes and where to find them in H-Mart.

## Potentially Hard-to-Find Ingredients

### Gochujang (Korean Red Pepper Paste)
- **Difficulty Level**: Moderate (Becoming more widely available)
- **Where to Find at H-Mart**: Sauce aisle
- **Suitable Substitutes**:
  1. Ssamjang - Another Korean paste made with fermented soybean, sesame seeds, and garlic
  2. Sriracha - Thai chili sauce (less sweet and complex than gochujang)
  3. Miso Paste + Chili Flakes - For umami flavor with added heat
  4. Harissa - North African chili paste (spicier, less sweet)
  5. Homemade version: Tomato paste + chili powder + sugar + soy sauce
  6. Ketchup + Soy Sauce + Sriracha - Quick emergency substitute

### Gochugaru (Korean Red Pepper Flakes)
- **Difficulty Level**: Moderate
- **Where to Find at H-Mart**: Spice section
- **Suitable Substitutes**:
  1. Regular crushed red pepper flakes (will be hotter and less smoky)
  2. Paprika + cayenne pepper (2:1 ratio)
  3. Aleppo pepper (similar color but milder)
  4. Chipotle powder (will add smokiness but different flavor profile)

### Doenjang (Korean Soybean Paste)
- **Difficulty Level**: Moderate
- **Where to Find at H-Mart**: Sauce aisle
- **Suitable Substitutes**:
  1. Japanese miso paste (milder, less funky flavor)
  2. Chinese yellow bean paste (less fermented taste)
  3. Miso paste + small amount of fish sauce (for added funk)

### Tteok (Korean Rice Cakes)
- **Difficulty Level**: Moderate to High
- **Where to Find at H-Mart**: Refrigerated or frozen section
- **Suitable Substitutes**:
  1. Rice noodles (different texture but similar flavor profile)
  2. Gnocchi (different flavor but similar chewy texture)
  3. Homemade version using rice flour and water

### Fernbrake/Gosari (Dried Bracken Fern)
- **Difficulty Level**: High
- **Where to Find at H-Mart**: Dried goods/specialty section
- **Suitable Substitutes**:
  1. Shiitake mushrooms (different flavor but similar texture)
  2. Wood ear mushrooms
  3. Spinach (completely different but can work in some recipes)

### Korean Radish (Mu)
- **Difficulty Level**: Moderate
- **Where to Find at H-Mart**: Produce section
- **Suitable Substitutes**:
  1. Daikon radish (Japanese variety, very similar)
  2. Red radishes (stronger flavor, use less)
  3. Jicama (milder flavor but similar crunch)

### Fish Cakes (Eomuk)
- **Difficulty Level**: Moderate to High
- **Where to Find at H-Mart**: Refrigerated or frozen section
- **Suitable Substitutes**:
  1. Japanese kamaboko or narutomaki
  2. Firm tofu (for vegetarian option)
  3. Surimi (imitation crab)

### Sweet Potato Starch Noodles (Dangmyeon)
- **Difficulty Level**: Moderate
- **Where to Find at H-Mart**: Noodle aisle
- **Suitable Substitutes**:
  1. Mung bean noodles (similar translucent appearance)
  2. Rice vermicelli (different texture but works in some recipes)
  3. Thin rice noodles

### Korean Soup Soy Sauce (Guk-Ganjang)
- **Difficulty Level**: Moderate
- **Where to Find at H-Mart**: Sauce aisle
- **Suitable Substitutes**:
  1. Regular soy sauce + dash of fish sauce
  2. Light soy sauce (will alter color slightly)
  3. Regular soy sauce diluted with a bit of water or broth

### Perilla Leaves (Kkaennip)
- **Difficulty Level**: High (Seasonal)
- **Where to Find at H-Mart**: Produce section (seasonal)
- **Suitable Substitutes**:
  1. Shiso leaves (Japanese variety, similar flavor)
  2. Basil leaves (different flavor but can work)
  3. Mint leaves (in some applications)

## Alternative Names and Packaging

Many Korean ingredients may be labeled under different names or in Korean characters. Here's a guide to help identify them:

- **Gochujang**: 고추장 - Look for red tubs or plastic containers
- **Gochugaru**: 고춧가루 - Usually in plastic bags or containers
- **Doenjang**: 된장 - Brown paste in plastic tubs
- **Tteok**: 떡 - Cylindrical white rice cakes
- **Dangmyeon**: 당면 - Clear, grayish dried noodles in packages
- **Guk-Ganjang**: 국간장 - Look for soup soy sauce or 조선간장 (Joseon ganjang)

## Frozen, Refrigerated, or Shelf-Stable Guide

| Ingredient | Section | Storage Notes |
|------------|---------|--------------|
| Gochujang | Shelf-stable | Refrigerate after opening |
| Gochugaru | Shelf-stable | Store in cool, dry place |
| Doenjang | Shelf-stable | Refrigerate after opening |
| Tteok (Rice Cakes) | Refrigerated/Frozen | Use quickly once thawed |
| Kimchi | Refrigerated | Will continue to ferment |
| Fish Cakes | Refrigerated/Frozen | Check expiration date |
| Sweet Potato Noodles | Shelf-stable | Store in cool, dry place |
| Perilla Leaves | Fresh produce | Use quickly, doesn't store well |
| Fernbrake/Gosari | Dried goods | Needs soaking before use |

## Gluten-Free Considerations

Some Korean ingredients traditionally contain wheat or other gluten sources:
- Commercial Gochujang often contains wheat
- Doenjang may contain wheat
- Soy sauce typically contains wheat

Look for specifically labeled gluten-free versions at H-Mart or consider these alternatives:
- Gluten-free gochujang brands are becoming more available
- Tamari can substitute for soy sauce
- 100% buckwheat soba noodles can substitute for some noodle dishes

This guide should help shoppers navigate H-Mart to find specific Korean ingredients or suitable substitutes when certain items are unavailable.
