# Korean Lunch Recipes Research (Part 2)

## Research Scope
- Researched additional Korean lunch recipes suitable for batch cooking
- Focused on recipes that are:
  - Easy to prepare (minimal cooking steps)
  - Suitable for batch cooking on Sundays
  - Can be stored and reheated throughout the week
  - Use ingredients commonly available at H-Mart
  - Different from previously documented recipes (Dakgangjeong and Kimchi Mandu)
- Specifically explored recipes that offer variety in flavors, ingredients, and textures

## Key Findings
- Many traditional Korean dishes are well-suited for batch cooking and weekly meal prep
- Dishes with sauce components maintain moisture and flavor when reheated
- Glass noodle dishes like Japchae can be stored for several days and even frozen
- Rice-based dishes like Bibimbap can be prepared in components and assembled fresh each day
- Proper storage techniques are crucial for maintaining food quality throughout the week
- Most Korean dishes reheat well on stovetop or microwave with minor adjustments

### Recipe 1: Bibimbap (비빔밥) - Korean Mixed Rice Bowl

#### Recipe Name
Bibimbap (비빔밥) - Korean Mixed Rice Bowl

#### Brief Description
Bibimbap is a popular Korean dish that combines rice, seasoned vegetables, meat, and a fried egg, all mixed with a spicy gochujang sauce. The name literally means "mixed rice." This dish is highly customizable and perfect for batch cooking as the components can be prepared separately and assembled fresh each day, making it ideal for weekly meal prep.

#### Preparation Time
30-40 minutes (for all components)

#### Cooking Time
30-40 minutes (for all components)

#### Ingredients
**For the rice base:**
- 4 cups cooked jasmine or short-grain rice

**For the vegetables (choose 4-5):**
- 6 cups fresh spinach
- 2 carrots, julienned
- 2 zucchini, julienned
- 1 cup bean sprouts
- 8 dried shiitake mushrooms (or fresh mushrooms of choice)
- 1 cucumber, julienned

**For the beef:**
- 1/2 lb ground beef or thinly sliced beef
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon brown sugar
- 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- 1 teaspoon minced garlic
- 1/2 teaspoon grated ginger
- 1/2 grated apple (for tenderizing)

**For the bibimbap sauce:**
- 4 tablespoons gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
- 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 1 tablespoon water
- 1 teaspoon vinegar
- 1 teaspoon minced garlic

**Additional toppings:**
- 4 eggs (for frying)
- Sesame seeds
- Kimchi (optional)
- Sliced green onions

#### Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions
1. **Prepare the rice:**
   - Cook rice according to package instructions and set aside.

2. **Prepare the vegetables:**
   - Spinach: Blanch spinach briefly in boiling water, drain, squeeze out excess water, and season with a pinch of salt, minced garlic, and sesame oil.
   - Carrots: Sauté julienned carrots with a pinch of salt until slightly softened, about 2-3 minutes.
   - Zucchini: Sauté julienned zucchini with a pinch of salt until slightly softened, about 2-3 minutes.
   - Bean sprouts: Blanch bean sprouts for 1-2 minutes, drain, and season with a pinch of salt, minced garlic, and sesame oil.
   - Mushrooms: If using dried shiitake, soak in warm water for 30 minutes, then slice and sauté with soy sauce and a pinch of sugar.
   - Cucumber: Season julienned cucumber with a pinch of salt and set aside.

3. **Cook the beef:**
   - Mix beef with soy sauce, brown sugar, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, and grated apple.
   - Heat a pan over medium-high heat and cook the beef until browned and cooked through, about 3-4 minutes.

4. **Make the bibimbap sauce:**
   - Mix all sauce ingredients in a small bowl until well combined.

5. **Fry the eggs:**
   - Heat a non-stick pan with a little oil and fry eggs sunny-side up.

6. **Assemble for immediate consumption:**
   - Place a portion of rice in a bowl.
   - Arrange the vegetables and beef on top of the rice.
   - Place a fried egg on top.
   - Serve with bibimbap sauce on the side or drizzled on top.
   - Sprinkle with sesame seeds and sliced green onions.

#### Storage Instructions
- Store each component separately in airtight containers in the refrigerator:
  - Cooked rice: 4-5 days
  - Prepared vegetables: 3-4 days
  - Cooked beef: 3-4 days
  - Bibimbap sauce: 1-2 weeks
- For meal prep, portion rice into individual containers and arrange vegetables and beef on top, but store the sauce separately.
- Do not pre-fry eggs; cook them fresh when ready to eat.

#### Reheating Instructions
- **For meal prep assembly:**
  1. Microwave the container with rice, vegetables, and beef for 1-2 minutes until hot.
  2. Fry a fresh egg and place on top.
  3. Add bibimbap sauce and mix everything together before eating.
- **Alternative method:**
  1. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the rice, vegetables, and beef.
  3. Heat until warmed through, stirring occasionally.
  4. Top with a freshly fried egg and sauce.

#### Nutritional Information
- Calories: Approximately 450-500 per serving
- Protein: 25g
- Carbohydrates: 60g
- Fat: 15g
- Fiber: 5g
- Sodium: 800mg

### Recipe 2: Japchae (잡채) - Korean Glass Noodle Stir-Fry

#### Recipe Name
Japchae (잡채) - Korean Glass Noodle Stir-Fry

#### Brief Description
Japchae is a popular Korean dish made with sweet potato glass noodles (dangmyeon) stir-fried with vegetables and meat in a savory-sweet sauce. It's traditionally served as a side dish or appetizer but can easily be a main course. The chewy texture of the glass noodles combined with the colorful vegetables makes this dish both visually appealing and delicious. Japchae is perfect for batch cooking as it stores well and maintains its flavor and texture when reheated.

#### Preparation Time
30 minutes

#### Cooking Time
20-25 minutes

#### Ingredients
**For the noodles:**
- 8 oz (250g) sweet potato glass noodles (dangmyeon)

**For the beef:**
- 8 oz (250g) beef (ribeye or sirloin), thinly sliced
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- 1 teaspoon sesame oil
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder (or 1 clove minced fresh garlic)

**For the vegetables:**
- 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
- 2 carrots, julienned
- 2 cups spinach
- 4-5 dried shiitake mushrooms, soaked and sliced (or 1 cup fresh mushrooms)
- 2-3 green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces
- Optional: 1/2 red bell pepper, julienned

**For the sauce:**
- 4 tablespoons soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons sugar
- 2 tablespoons sesame oil
- 1 tablespoon minced garlic
- 1 teaspoon roasted sesame seeds
- Black pepper to taste

#### Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions
1. **Prepare the noodles:**
   - Boil 8-10 cups of water in a large pot.
   - Add the glass noodles and cook according to package instructions (usually 6-8 minutes) until soft but still chewy.
   - Drain and rinse under cold water.
   - Cut the noodles with scissors into shorter, more manageable lengths.
   - Toss with 1 tablespoon of sesame oil to prevent sticking and set aside.

2. **Marinate the beef:**
   - In a bowl, combine the beef with soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, and garlic powder.
   - Let it marinate while preparing the vegetables.

3. **Prepare the vegetables:**
   - If using dried shiitake mushrooms, soak them in warm water for 30 minutes, then slice.
   - Blanch the spinach briefly in boiling water, drain, squeeze out excess water, and season with a pinch of salt and a drizzle of sesame oil.
   - Julienne the carrots, slice the onions, and cut the green onions.

4. **Cook the ingredients separately:**
   - In a large pan or wok, heat 1 tablespoon of oil over medium-high heat.
   - Cook the marinated beef until browned, about 3-4 minutes. Remove and set aside.
   - In the same pan, sauté the onions until translucent.
   - Add the carrots and cook for 2-3 minutes until slightly softened.
   - Add the mushrooms and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
   - Add the green onions and cook for 1 minute.
   - Remove all vegetables and set aside.

5. **Combine and season:**
   - In the same pan, add the cooked noodles, beef, and vegetables.
   - Add the sauce ingredients (soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, garlic).
   - Stir-fry everything together for 2-3 minutes until well combined and heated through.
   - Add the blanched spinach and toss gently.
   - Sprinkle with sesame seeds and black pepper.

#### Storage Instructions
- Cool completely before storing.
- Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4-5 days.
- Japchae can also be frozen for up to 3 months in freezer-safe containers or bags.

#### Reheating Instructions
- **Refrigerated japchae:**
  - Stovetop method (preferred): Reheat in a non-stick frying pan over medium-low heat, stirring often. Add a small splash of water if it seems dry.
  - Microwave method: Heat for 2-3 minutes, stirring halfway through.
- **Frozen japchae:**
  - Thaw in the refrigerator overnight.
  - Reheat using one of the methods above.

#### Nutritional Information
- Calories: Approximately 350-400 per serving
- Protein: 15g
- Carbohydrates: 50g
- Fat: 12g
- Fiber: 3g
- Sodium: 700mg
(Values are approximate and may vary based on specific ingredients used)

### Recipe 3: Tteokbokki (떡볶이) - Spicy Korean Rice Cakes

#### Recipe Name
Tteokbokki (떡볶이) - Spicy Korean Rice Cakes

#### Brief Description
Tteokbokki is a popular Korean street food made with chewy rice cakes (tteok) cooked in a spicy, sweet red pepper sauce. This comforting dish is beloved for its unique texture and bold flavors. While traditionally served fresh, tteokbokki can be prepared in advance and reheated throughout the week, making it suitable for batch cooking with some adjustments to the storage and reheating process.

#### Preparation Time
15 minutes

#### Cooking Time
20 minutes

#### Ingredients
**For the rice cakes:**
- 1 lb (500g) Korean rice cakes (cylindrical type)
- 4 cups water or anchovy stock (for more flavor)

**For the sauce:**
- 3 tablespoons gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
- 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes) - adjust to taste
- 3 tablespoons sugar
- 3 tablespoons soy sauce
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon sesame oil

**Additional ingredients:**
- 1 small onion, sliced
- 2 green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces
- Optional: 2 hard-boiled eggs
- Optional: 1/2 package fish cakes (eomuk), cut into pieces
- Optional: 1 cup cabbage, roughly chopped

**Garnish:**
- Sesame seeds
- Chopped green onions

#### Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions
1. **Prepare the rice cakes:**
   - If using frozen rice cakes, thaw them completely.
   - Soak the rice cakes in cold water for 10-15 minutes, then drain.

2. **Make the sauce:**
   - In a bowl, mix gochujang, gochugaru, sugar, soy sauce, minced garlic, and sesame oil until well combined.

3. **Cook the tteokbokki:**
   - In a large, wide pan, bring the water or anchovy stock to a boil.
   - Add the sliced onion and cook for 2 minutes.
   - Add the sauce mixture and stir until dissolved.
   - Add the rice cakes and fish cakes (if using) and cook over medium heat for about 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
   - When the rice cakes are soft and the sauce has thickened, add the green onions and cabbage (if using) and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
   - Add the hard-boiled eggs (if using) and heat through.

4. **Finish and serve:**
   - Garnish with sesame seeds and additional chopped green onions.
   - Serve hot.

#### Storage Instructions
- For best results, store the rice cakes and sauce separately:
  - Cook the rice cakes until just slightly undercooked.
  - Drain and rinse with cold water to stop the cooking process.
  - Store the rice cakes in an airtight container with a small amount of water to prevent sticking.
  - Store the sauce separately in another container.
- If storing together, tteokbokki can be refrigerated for 2-3 days in an airtight container.
- Note: The rice cakes will continue to absorb the sauce during storage, becoming softer and less chewy over time.

#### Reheating Instructions
- **If stored separately:**
  1. Heat the sauce in a pan until it starts to simmer.
  2. Add the rice cakes and a splash of water.
  3. Cook over medium heat until the rice cakes are heated through and have reached desired chewiness.
- **If stored together:**
  1. Place in a pan with 2-4 tablespoons of water.
  2. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add more water if the sauce is too thick.
  4. Alternatively, microwave with a splash of water for 2-3 minutes, stirring halfway through.

#### Nutritional Information
- Calories: Approximately 300-350 per serving
- Protein: 7g
- Carbohydrates: 65g
- Fat: 5g
- Fiber: 2g
- Sodium: 900mg
(Values are approximate and may vary based on specific ingredients used)
