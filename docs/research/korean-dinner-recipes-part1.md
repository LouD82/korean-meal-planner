# Korean Dinner Recipes Research (Part 1)

## Research Scope
- Research focused on Korean dinner recipes suitable for batch cooking
- Specifically looking for recipes that are:
  - Easy to prepare (minimal cooking steps)
  - Suitable for batch cooking on Sundays
  - Can be stored and reheated throughout the week
  - Use ingredients commonly available at H-Mart
  - Different from the lunch recipes already documented
  - More substantial for dinner options

## Key Findings
- Ground beef-based Korean dishes are particularly well-suited for batch cooking and reheating
- Dishes with sauce components maintain moisture and flavor when reheated
- Proper storage techniques are crucial for maintaining food quality throughout the week
- Some Korean stews can be refrigerated but not frozen due to texture changes in ingredients
- Meal prep containers with compartments help maintain the integrity of different components
- Recipes can often be modified to include additional vegetables for a more balanced meal

### Recipe 1: Korean Beef Bowl Meal Prep

#### Recipe Name
Korean Beef Bowl Meal Prep

#### Brief Description
This Korean Beef Bowl Meal Prep features ground beef cooked in a sweet and savory sauce, served with brown rice, sautéed spinach, and hard-boiled eggs. It's a balanced, protein-rich meal that's perfect for batch cooking and can be enjoyed throughout the week. The dish offers the flavors of Korean BBQ in a convenient, portioned meal prep format.

#### Preparation Time
15 minutes

#### Cooking Time
30 minutes

#### Ingredients
**For the rice base:**
- 1 cup brown rice
- 2 cups water

**For the Korean beef:**
- 1 pound ground beef
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- ¼ cup brown sugar, packed
- ¼ cup reduced sodium soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon freshly grated ginger
- 2 teaspoons sesame oil
- ½ teaspoon Sriracha, or more to taste
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 2 green onions, thinly sliced
- ¼ teaspoon sesame seeds

**For the sides:**
- 4 large eggs
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 4 cups chopped spinach

#### Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions
1. **Cook the rice:**
   - In a large saucepan filled with 2 cups of water, cook rice according to package instructions; set aside.

2. **Prepare the hard-boiled eggs:**
   - Place eggs in a large saucepan and cover with cold water by 1 inch.
   - Bring to a boil and cook for 1 minute.
   - Cover eggs with a tight-fitting lid and remove from heat; set aside for 8-10 minutes.
   - Drain well and let cool before peeling and slicing in half.

3. **Prepare the spinach:**
   - Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
   - Add garlic and cook, stirring frequently, until fragrant, about 1-2 minutes.
   - Stir in spinach until wilted, about 2-3 minutes; set aside.

4. **Make the sauce for the beef:**
   - In a small bowl, whisk together brown sugar, soy sauce, ginger, sesame oil, and Sriracha.

5. **Cook the ground beef:**
   - Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
   - Add garlic and cook, stirring constantly, until fragrant, about 1 minute.
   - Add ground beef and cook until browned, about 3-5 minutes, making sure to crumble the beef as it cooks; drain excess fat.
   - Stir in soy sauce mixture and green onions until well combined, allowing to simmer until heated through, about 2 minutes.

6. **Assemble the meal prep containers:**
   - Place rice, eggs, spinach, and ground beef mixture into meal prep containers.
   - Garnish with green onion and sesame seeds, if desired.

#### Storage Instructions
- Store in airtight meal prep containers in the refrigerator for up to 4-5 days.
- Keep the containers sealed until ready to eat to maintain freshness.
- For best results, use containers with compartments to keep components separate until eating.

#### Reheating Instructions
- Microwave: Remove the lid and microwave for 2-3 minutes until heated through, stirring halfway if possible.
- Stovetop: For best results, reheat the beef mixture in a small pan over medium heat until warmed through (about 3-4 minutes), and microwave the rice and vegetables separately.
- If the beef mixture seems dry after storage, add a tablespoon of water before reheating.

#### Nutritional Information
- Calories: Approximately 450 per serving
- Protein: 30g
- Carbohydrates: 40g
- Fat: 20g
- Fiber: 5g
- Sodium: 650mg
(Values are approximate and may vary based on specific ingredients used)

### Recipe 2: Korean Beef Bulgogi Meal Prep

#### Recipe Name
Korean Beef Bulgogi Meal Prep

#### Brief Description
This Korean Beef Bulgogi Meal Prep features ground beef marinated in a traditional bulgogi sauce with mushrooms and onions, served with rice and vegetables. It's a flavorful, protein-rich meal that's perfect for batch cooking and can be portioned for multiple meals throughout the week. The dish offers authentic Korean flavors in a convenient, meal prep format.

#### Preparation Time
20 minutes (plus 15 minutes to overnight for marinating)

#### Cooking Time
25 minutes

#### Ingredients
**For the rice base:**
- 2.5 cups uncooked short-grain white rice
- 2.5 cups water

**For the beef bulgogi:**
- 2.2 lb (1kg) ground beef
- 1 large yellow onion, finely diced
- 3-4 green onions, thinly sliced (divided for cooking and garnish)
- 5 white button mushrooms (about 2.5 oz/70g), finely chopped
- 1 mild green chili, finely chopped (optional)
- 1 mild red chili, finely chopped (optional)
- 3 tbsp neutral-tasting oil
- 1.5 tbsp toasted sesame oil
- Generous pinch of toasted sesame seeds, to garnish

**For the bulgogi marinade:**
- 5 tbsp soy sauce
- 3 tbsp sugar
- 4 tbsp mirin
- 4 tbsp oyster sauce
- 3 tbsp minced garlic
- 2 tbsp toasted sesame oil
- Black pepper, to taste
- Small pinch of MSG (optional)

**For the vegetables:**
- 1-2 broccoli heads (or asparagus, string beans, etc.)
- Kosher salt, to season
- Kimchi (optional), to serve

#### Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions
1. **Cook the rice:**
   - Wash the rice to remove any dust and debris. Drain and repeat two or three times.
   - Add the same amount of water to the bowl and cook in a rice cooker.

2. **Marinate the ground beef:**
   - Prepare the vegetables: Finely dice the onion. Thinly slice the green onions and save some for garnish. Finely chop the mushrooms. Finely chop the chili peppers (if using).
   - Make the bulgogi marinade: In a small container, mix together the soy sauce, sugar, mirin, oyster sauce, minced garlic, sesame oil, black pepper, and MSG (if using).
   - To a large mixing bowl, add the ground beef, mushrooms, and marinade. Mix well. Cover and marinate in the fridge for at least 15 minutes or overnight.

3. **Prepare the broccoli:**
   - Cut off the florets and cut them into smaller bite-sized pieces.
   - Bring a pot of water to a boil. Once it comes to a boil, lightly season with salt and add the broccoli.
   - Blanch for 1 to 2 minutes. Shock them in some ice water. Drain well.

4. **Cook the ground beef bulgogi:**
   - In a large wok (or pan), heat the oil over medium-high heat.
   - Once heated, add the white parts of the green onions and onion. Sauté for 5 minutes or until the onion becomes translucent.
   - Add the beef mixture. Spread it out and give it some time to caramelize.
   - Once you've got some sear on the bottom, flip and break it into small pieces.
   - Keep stirring it around until the beef is cooked through, about 12 minutes.
   - Turn the heat off. Add the chili peppers (if using), the rest of the green onions, and the sesame oil. Give it a final mix.

5. **Assemble the meal prep containers:**
   - Divide the rice into microwave-safe containers.
   - Make a barrier with broccoli. (Make sure the broccoli is really well drained.)
   - Add the ground beef bulgogi.
   - Sprinkle with sesame seeds.
   - Optionally, serve with kimchi on the side.

#### Storage Instructions
- The bulgogi meal prep will last up to 3 days in the refrigerator.
- For longer storage, you can freeze portions for a few months.
- Make sure to cool completely before storing.
- If you don't want to cook it all at once, you can divide the marinated beef into portions and store them in the fridge or freezer.

#### Reheating Instructions
- Microwave: Heat the container with rice, vegetables, and beef for 2-3 minutes until hot.
- If frozen, thaw in the refrigerator overnight before reheating.
- Add a tablespoon of water before reheating if the dish seems dry.

#### Nutritional Information
For 6 servings:
- Calories: 738 per serving
- Protein: 55g
- Carbohydrates: 68g
- Fat: 25g

For 7 servings:
- Calories: 633 per serving
- Protein: 47g
- Carbohydrates: 58g
- Fat: 21g
(Values are approximate and may vary based on specific ingredients used)

### Recipe 3: Kimchi Jjigae (Kimchi Stew)

#### Recipe Name
Kimchi Jjigae (김치찌개) - Kimchi Stew

#### Brief Description
Kimchi Jjigae is a traditional Korean stew made with fermented kimchi, tofu, and pork belly, simmered in a spicy, savory broth. This comforting dish is a staple in Korean homes and makes for a hearty dinner option. While it's traditionally served fresh, it can be prepared in advance and reheated throughout the week, making it suitable for batch cooking with some adjustments to the storage process.

#### Preparation Time
15 minutes

#### Cooking Time
30 minutes

#### Ingredients
- 2 cups (about 300g) kimchi, preferably old and sour, cut into bite-size pieces
- 1/2 cup kimchi juice (from the kimchi container)
- 1/2 pound (about 230g) pork belly or shoulder, cut into bite-size pieces
- 1 block (about 14 oz/400g) soft tofu (sundubu), sliced
- 1 medium onion, sliced
- 2-3 green onions, chopped
- 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
- 1 tablespoon gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
- 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance the sour taste)
- 1 teaspoon sesame oil
- 2-3 cups water or anchovy broth (for more flavor)
- Salt to taste
- 1 egg (optional)

#### Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions
1. **Prepare the ingredients:**
   - Cut the kimchi into bite-size pieces.
   - Cut the pork into bite-size pieces.
   - Slice the tofu about 1/2-inch thick.
   - Roughly chop the green onions.

2. **Cook the kimchi and pork:**
   - In a pot or deep pan, add the kimchi, kimchi juice, and pork.
   - Cook over medium-high heat for about 5-7 minutes until the pork starts to cook and the kimchi becomes softer.
   - This step helps develop extra flavor.

3. **Add the seasonings:**
   - Add the minced garlic, gochugaru, gochujang, and sugar (if using).
   - Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

4. **Add the liquid and simmer:**
   - Pour in the water or anchovy broth.
   - Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to medium-low.
   - Cover and simmer for about 15 minutes until the pork is fully cooked and tender.

5. **Add the remaining ingredients:**
   - Add the sliced tofu and onion.
   - Continue to simmer for another 5-7 minutes until the tofu is heated through and the onion is softened.
   - Add the sesame oil and adjust the seasoning with salt if needed.

6. **Finish the stew:**
   - If using an egg, crack it into the center of the stew.
   - Cover and cook for another 1-2 minutes until the egg is cooked to your preference.
   - Garnish with chopped green onions.
   - Serve hot with a bowl of rice.

#### Storage Instructions
- Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days.
- For best results, store the stew without the egg and add a fresh egg when reheating.
- The stew may become more sour as it sits in the refrigerator due to the continued fermentation of the kimchi.
- Not recommended for freezing as the freezer will extract moisture from the tofu and dilute the broth, affecting the texture and flavor.

#### Reheating Instructions
- Stovetop (preferred method): Transfer to a pot and reheat over medium heat until hot, about 5-7 minutes. Add a little water if the stew has thickened too much.
- Microwave: Heat for 2-3 minutes until hot, stirring halfway through.
- If desired, add a fresh egg during reheating for the best texture.

#### Nutritional Information
- Calories: Approximately 350-400 per serving
- Protein: 20g
- Carbohydrates: 15g
- Fat: 25g
- Fiber: 3g
- Sodium: 900mg
(Values are approximate and may vary based on specific ingredients used)
